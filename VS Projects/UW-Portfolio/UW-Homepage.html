Journey To UW

Why CS, why now.

I’ve spent the last few years experimenting with computers, small projects, and whatever ideas I could get my hands on, and somewhere along the way I realized that this is the work that actually makes me feel engaged. I’m not choosing computer science because it’s convenient or easy, it won’t be but because it’s the one path where I can see myself growing, learning, and building something meaningful. I’m still early in my journey, still figuring things out, but I’m committed to developing a real foundation: algorithms, programming, problem‑solving, and the discipline it takes to turn curiosity into skill.

Why UW, why seattle.

The University of Washington feels like the right place for me — not just because of the Paul G. Allen School and the opportunities it offers, but because I want to build a life in the Pacific Northwest. The mountains, the evergreen landscape, the atmosphere around Seattle all feel like where I want to grow into adulthood. UW represents a gateway to that future: a place where I can challenge myself academically, deepen my understanding of computer science, and eventually settle into a stable, meaningful career. My goal isn’t to chase something flashy; it’s to build a secure life doing work I care about, and UW is the stepping stone that makes that possible.
